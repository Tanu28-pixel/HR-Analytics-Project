Employee Attrition Insights
The organization records an overall employee attrition rate of approximately 16.12%.
Employee attrition is one of the major workforce challenges identified through dashboard analysis.
Attrition trends indicate strong influence from overtime, job satisfaction, and years at company.

Department Insights
Research & Development department shows the highest employee attrition among all departments.
Sales department also contributes significantly to workforce turnover.
Human Resources department contains the lowest employee count comparatively.

Overtime Insights
Employees working overtime demonstrate noticeably higher attrition trends.
Work-life balance appears to play an important role in employee retention.
Overtime analysis highlights potential workforce stress and workload imbalance.

Job Satisfaction Insights
Employee retention is strongly influenced by job satisfaction levels.
Employees with lower satisfaction scores contribute more to attrition patterns.
Moderate satisfaction levels are more common across the organization.

Age Group Insights
Majority of employees belong to the 26–35 years age group.
Attrition is more common among younger and less experienced employees.
Mid-career professionals form the largest portion of the workforce.

Salary Insights
Average salary differs across departments and job roles.
Research & Development department records comparatively higher salary distribution.
Salary variations may influence employee retention and satisfaction.

Gender Insights
Male employees contribute a slightly higher attrition count compared to female employees.
Gender distribution across the organization is not completely balanced.
Attrition patterns are observed across both genders.

Attrition Trend Insights
Employees with fewer years at company show higher turnover trends.
Attrition decreases gradually with increasing employee experience.
Workforce stability improves among long-term employees.

Overall Business Insights
Employee attrition is impacted by multiple factors including overtime, salary, satisfaction, and department workload.
HR analytics dashboards help organizations identify workforce challenges and improve retention strategies.
Business intelligence visualization supports better workforce planning and employee management decisions.
